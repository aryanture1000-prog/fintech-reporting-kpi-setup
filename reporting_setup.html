The Situation
❌ What the startup had before: A Series A payments fintech operating across the US and EU had no structured financial reporting. Revenue was tracked in a Google Sheet, expenses scattered across Stripe and QuickBooks, and USD/EUR figures were never reconciled. Their Series B pitch was 5 months away and investors would demand CAC, LTV, burn multiple, runway, and MRR growth — none of which they could answer cleanly. The founding team was flying blind.
✅ What was delivered: A full financial reporting system — a KPI framework tuned to their business model, three report types for different audiences (ops team, leadership, investors), and a Python-automated monthly generation pipeline. Every month-end now produces three clean, currency-normalized reports in under 15 minutes.

Step 3 — How It's Automated
1
Data Pull

Python script connects to Stripe and QuickBooks APIs at month-end. All transactions pulled automatically — no manual CSV exports needed from the finance team.

2
FX Normalization

All EUR amounts converted to USD at ECB reference rates for the last business day of the month. Original EUR figures stored in a separate column for full audit trail.

3
KPI Calculation

All 16 KPIs calculated automatically from the cleaned, normalized data. Each one compared to prior month and to target — RAG status assigned programmatically.

4
Report Generation

Three reports output as formatted Excel files plus a PDF summary — ready to send to each audience without any manual reformatting or copy-pasting.

5
Monthly Runtime

Total time from script trigger to three completed reports: under 15 minutes. Replaces what previously took two full working days every single month.
